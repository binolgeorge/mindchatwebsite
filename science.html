THE TECHNOLOGY

EEG: A Window into Mental Health

Electroencephalography (EEG) measures electrical activity in the brain, offering valuable insights into mental states such as stress, anxiety, and depression.

How EEG Works in MindChat

MindChat integrates with compatible EEG headsets like Muse 2 for non-invasive brainwave monitoring. While not a diagnostic tool, EEG signals offer objective physiological data points. These can correlate with changes in mental state (like stress or mood shifts), acting as potential indicators alongside self-reports.

Alpha Asymmetry Detection

Variations in alpha wave activity between the brain's hemispheres are associated with mood states like anxiety and depression. MindChat algorithms detect these patterns to provide valuable insights.

Beta Activity Monitoring

Elevated beta waves (13–30 Hz) reliably track acute stress and hyper-arousal, so MindChat relies on beta metrics for real-time stress or anxiety alerts. Gamma (>30 Hz) is displayed only when high-quality data are available and should be considered exploratory due to hardware noise constraints.

Important Considerations:

Consumer EEG devices like Muse are sensitive to artifacts (muscle tension, eye blinks). MindChat employs signal processing techniques, but results are most reliable under calm conditions. EEG patterns show correlations, not causation, and interpretation requires context. Gamma band data (>30Hz) is highly susceptible to noise on consumer hardware and should be viewed as exploratory.

Why MUSE?

We selected the Muse 2 based on independent studies indicating its capability to capture key metrics like frontal alpha asymmetry and basic spectral power (alpha, beta, theta) comparable to some research systems under specific conditions1. This allows MindChat to analyze relevant correlates of mood and stress, while our algorithms work to mitigate potential noise.

Source: 1 Krigolson et al., 2017 Front Neurosci; Rogala et al., 2020 Front Hum Neurosci.

EEG Biomarkers Associated with Concussion

Following head impacts, concussions can cause measurable changes in the brain's electrical activity. MindChat analyzes EEG data for patterns that have been associated with such neurological disruptions in research literature:

Power Spectrum Analysis

Concussion research often associates impacts with changes in EEG power, such as potential increases in slow-wave activity (delta/theta) and decreases in faster waves (alpha/beta). MindChat analyzes the power spectrum for patterns that may correlate with these reported post-impact changes, offering data points for consideration alongside clinical signs.

Functional Connectivity Patterns

Research suggests concussions can alter typical communication patterns (functional connectivity) between brain regions. MindChat evaluates connectivity metrics, looking for deviations from baseline or expected patterns that have been associated with post-concussion states in scientific literature.

Task-Related Activity Analysis

MindChat analyzes brain responses during specific cognitive tasks (when available). Changes in these response patterns can sometimes indicate altered cognitive processing speed or attention, which are known potential correlates following concussion.

Why Objective Data Matters

Because concussion symptoms are not always immediately apparent, objective EEG data captured soon after a potential impact can provide valuable supplementary information to aid awareness and support timely implementation of appropriate management protocols.

BRAIN ACTIVITY

Understanding Brainwaves

Explore the different brainwave types and their associated mental states that MindChat monitors to provide insights into your mental well-being.

Delta

0.5–4 Hz

Associated with deep sleep and restorative processes. These slow waves are essential for physical healing and recovery.

Theta

4–8 Hz

Present during meditation, creativity, and daydreaming. Theta waves facilitate memory formation and emotional processing.

Alpha

8–12 Hz

Dominant during relaxation and calmness. Alpha waves are indicators of mental relaxation and reduced anxiety states.

Beta

12–30 Hz

Prevalent during active thinking, focus, and stress. Elevated beta activity often correlates with mental strain and anxiety.

Gamma

30–100 Hz

Linked to high-level information processing. Because Muse sensors are noisy above 30 Hz, MindChat reports gamma only as an exploratory trend.

UNDERSTANDING INSIGHTS

Alert Interpretation Guide

Learn what each alert means and how to take appropriate action. MindChat issues different alerts based on specific brainwave patterns it detects, informed by scientific literature. For example:

Stress / Amxiety Alert

High Beta wave activity detected

What It Means

Elevated beta waves are often associated with increased mental activity, which can include stress or focused concentration. This pattern suggests a state of heightened alertness or cognitive load.

Suggested Actions

Try guided visualization or meditation techniques

Mood Regulation Alert

Frontal Alpha asymmetry detected

What It Means

Research links significant frontal alpha asymmetry with tendencies toward certain mood regulation patterns, sometimes associated with anxiety or depression risk. This is an indicator for awareness, not a diagnosis.

Suggested Actions

Engage in grounding techniques like the 5-4-3-2-1 exercise

Practice progressive muscle relaxation

Depression Risk Alert

Pattern of frontal alpha asymmetry detected

What It Means

Studies associate sustained frontal alpha asymmetry with increased vulnerability to depressive mood states. Consider this alert a prompt for self-reflection and potential consultation with a professional if concerns persist. It is not a clinical diagnosis.

Suggested Actions

Keep a journal of your feelings and moods

Engage in activities that bring joy or fulfillment

Consider professional support for ongoing assistance

Concussion Alert

EEG biomarker changes detected

What It Means

Significant changes in certain EEG biomarkers (like power spectrum or connectivity) can be associated with neurological disruption following impact. These are objective data points to consider alongside clinical assessment, especially as EEG changes may occur even without obvious symptoms.

Suggested Actions

Consult a healthcare provider for proper evaluation

Avoid returning to physical activity until cleared

Monitor symptoms and continue tracking with MindChat

COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about MindChat's technology, usage, and privacy.

Is MindChat a replacement for therapy?

No, MindChat is a supportive tool designed to raise awareness and provide insights. It is not a substitute for professional mental health services. We recommend using MindChat alongside professional care when needed.

How should I interpret an alert?

Alerts indicate potential changes in mental state but are not diagnostic. Use them as prompts for self-care and consult professionals if concerns persist. They are designed to increase your awareness and suggest possible actions, not to diagnose conditions.

How accurate are concussion detections?

MindChat's concussion feature is based on emerging EEG research and offers timely monitoring to support awareness. While it provides useful insights, it is not a diagnostic tool and has not yet undergone formal clinical validation. All suspected concussions should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

How often should I use MindChat?

Regular use (e.g., once or twice daily for stress, once a week for anxiety, and once every 2 weeks for depression) helps track mental health patterns over time and provides more reliable trend detection over time. For concussion monitoring, we recommend baseline measurements when healthy, and then as recommended by your healthcare provider following any potential head injury.

How does MindChat protect my data?

Your privacy is paramount. We use encryption and secure storage solutions to protect your data, complying with all relevant data protection regulations. Users have complete control over their data, with clear options to opt in or out of data sharing. MindChat follows HIPAA guidelines for healthcare data management.
